Journey with design systems

My first rendezvous with design systems

My journey with design systems started when I was working as a UI developer in Thoughtworks in 2016.

At that time, design systems was not something big in the market. But, I always felt that need of reusability and consistency that drove me to build a static style guide in PDF format for my team.

It was a simple document that collated all the foundational styles and components at one place. This helped our team to reduce inconsistencies whilst building new UI interfaces for our app which consequently accelerated our delivery.

My first ever style guide as a developer

Airline neutral: In the direction of modularity, reusability and scalability

In this project, I partnered with the Product Managers to plan and execute the rebranding of an eminent airlines which was acquired by another airlines.

We worked on building design directives with an external design agency. In the mobile web team, I lead the rebranding of the app. This involved:

  • Advocating and sharing the rebranding plan
  • Refactoring the SASS code to make it modular and consummable by other developers
  • pairing with other developers and QEs to enable them

In this project, the development teams (60+ developers in iOS, Android and Mobile web) and the QE team were our users, so delivering artifacts that is understandable and consumable by them was a crucial thing to do. This project helped me to understand the real power of design systems and the huge impact it can have.

Living Pattern Library

In 2017, I was working in Activate Media (a start-up incubator) as a designer. As the aim of the development team was to deliver quickly and in less time, I proposed to build a Living Pattern Library which will reduce the back forth between design and development teams and accelerate development.

Working as a sole designer in the team, I built foundational styles and UI components on Sketch. Inspired by the ReactJS storybook, I built a living pattern library on ReactJS which helped the developers to build user interfaces in less time.

The most amazing part was, the living pattern library got updated automatically as soon as any style or component was updated in the main application. So, it was the source of truth always, there were no extra efforts needed to update the pattern library.

MEP Design systems

In 2018, I worked for the Macmillan Education team in Springer Nature as a UX/UI Designer. I got the opportunity there to plan the designs systems project as well as execute it.

I worked with a fellow UI designer and a Lead UX designer to build the design systems for Macmillan Education Platform. With the given constraints, we started by deploying the design systems on Google Pages, so that design team can start consuming it.

Learnings and Challenges

Every project helped me to push my boundaries and learn more. There were many challenges related to the scalability, timelines, team availability, etc.

We overcame challenges by incorporating the design process in various projects. Constantly communicating and collaborating with cross-functional teams, conducting various workshops for alignment, getting feedback and most importantly building empathy helped us to move forward.
