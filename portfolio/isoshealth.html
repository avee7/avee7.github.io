Isoshealth Web platform

UX design UI design UI Development

Isoshealth aims at building a web platform which provides interactive online sessions with a team of leading healthcare professionals.

It helps to achieve your goals with THE POWER OF 3 - Dietician, Physiotherapist, Psychologist.

My Role

Working as a sole digital designer under the Creative Director helped me to enhance my ownership skills and gave me an opportunity to do the end-to-end design process.

My responsibilities in the project were:

Ideation & Analysis

Partnered with our business stakeholders to uncover insights and translate concepts into features.

Interaction Design

Translated the ideas and user journeys into paper mockups, then digital prototypes.

Living Style Guide

Created a living style guide that helped the design and development team to be on the same page.

User Testing

Partnered with the Creative Director to carry out user testing sessions to refine our product.

Templates

Coded the view using HTML and SASS and handed over the templates to the development team.

Front-end Tech Stack

Partnered with the developers to lay down the front-end tech stack using SASS and React.js.

The Project

Achieve your health goals by the 'power of 3'

We aimed to build an easy-to-use web interface that would allow:

  • clients and health care practitioners to have effective video consultations
  • clients to choose a dedicated team and carry out online consultations according to their preferred time

Our Approach

The expectation was to get the product out in the market within 3-4 months, so we decided to go for lean approach that included rapid sketching, designing quick mockups, prototyping and incorporating user feedback.

The Process

Understanding the User

We began the product discovery by understanding the user. A survey was carried out to understand questions related to the user's lifestyle, physical and mental health, emotional wellbeing, health goals, motivations, etc. A number of brainstorming sessions were carried out to understand aspects like:

  • what the users want to achieve
  • what are the business expectations
  • user personas
  • how users would use the application, etc.

Competitor Analysis

A competitor analysis on some products helped us to understand:

  • core features they offer
  • what are their product strengths
  • what are the issues they are not able to resolve.

Crafting the experience

After gathering insights from the previous phase, we assessed how best the features that the business aim to implement would add to the user experience of the product and help achieve their goals and their users’ goals.

We came up with various users personas and then crafted user journeys for each of our personas to conceptualise the proposed functionality.

Example of the client journey: Platform onboarding and joining

Sketching

We explored various ideas for layout, navigation and interaction by sketching and creating paper mockups.

We went through these ideas and weighed them against our requirements, design patterns, basic design principles and our constraints to decide on the best.

Design and Interaction

We went through the brand guidelines and designed the visuals of few key screens using appropriate colours, typography, and icons in Sketch.

After presenting those screens to the clients, we got quick feedback on the visual style and then designed the rest of the screens.

Next, we created interactive prototypes in Invision which helped us to get valuable feedback by user testing. The prototypes also helped to get feedback and approval from clients and the senior leadership.

Designing the product: Sketch, Creating interactive prototypes: Invision

Coding templates

After incorporating feedback from the users, clients and seniors, templates in HTML and SASS were created to deliver to the development team.

Continuous communication and collaboration between the designers and developers helped us to discuss ideas and concerns which, consequently, removed many blockers while development.

User Testing

We invited some practitioners and carried out user testing on our development environment. We got some good insights which helped us to further refine the overall experience of the application.
