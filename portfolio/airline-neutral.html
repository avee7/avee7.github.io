Airline Neutral

UI design Strategy and Planning UI Development

This project was built for Fly Delta Airlines and its newly acquired airlines, Virgin Atlantic

The mobile web, iOS and Android apps of Virgin Atlantic were rebranded based on the Fly Delta airlines visual guidelines.

My Role

Analysis

Analysed the Delta Airlines designs with the business analyst and product owner to plan the course of action.

Strategy

Devised a strategy to build the UI design for an airline neutral product

UI Design

Lead the implementation of UI design across iOS, Android and Mobile Web with developers

UI development

Lead and implemented SASS code to build a scalable solution across platforms

Project

Building an Airline neutral product

The objective of the airline-neutral project was to create a versatile product that could adapt to various visual styles, depending on the specific sub-product.

The project had two main aims:

  • Design a user interface that aligns with the Delta airlines' visual styles for mobile web, iOS, and Android applications.
  • Develop an airline-neutral solution based on the Delta airlines' framework, capable of accommodating any number of airlines in the future.

Approach and Planning

We devised an inclusive approach that empowered multidisciplinary teams (Mobile web, iOS, and Android) to advance in their individual directions while adhering to a set of directives.

Our primary goal was to ensure development teams comprehended the rationale behind the UI design, facilitating confident and efficient development.

Collaborating closely with the product owner and business analyst, we strategically organised the project into four distinct phases to achieve success.

Four steps of our planning

The Process

Problem Analysis

During the analysis phase, we thoroughly examined our existing designs and code base. Our focus was on guiding the development teams (mobile web, iOS, and Android) to review their code base and initiate the process of modularising and enhancing reusability.

Additionally, we conducted an in-depth analysis of the Delta Airlines design to define its visual styles and language. This step was crucial in establishing a cohesive and consistent approach to the design and development of the project.

Building Directives

Following the analysis, we proceeded to create directives by designing representative screens for all platforms. These screens served as a foundation for establishing a cohesive visual design for the new product, adhering to the brand guidelines.

We also developed logical transformations between the parent product and the sub-product, maintaining consistent user journeys while incorporating brand colours, components, and patterns. An example of this logical transformation involved leveraging foundational styles and components of the design.

In this phase, we ensured that the new product would align seamlessly with the parent brand, resulting in a unified and engaging user experience across all platforms.

Identifying and Fixing Inconsistencies

Taking on the role of a UI developer, I initiated the process of refactoring the mobile web code to enhance its modularity and reusability.

During this phase, we diligently identified and eliminated inconsistent styles and components, streamlining our directives. Additionally, we converged redundant styles, further refining the overall design approach.

For instance, we addressed the issue of having six different shades of primary blue and consolidated them into a single, consistent shade

By rectifying these inconsistencies, we significantly improved the codebase's clarity, efficiency, and adherence to the established design system. This meticulous approach ensured a more cohesive and polished user interface across all platforms.

Extract Structure

During the project, our primary objective was to distinguish between the product's structure and its visual appearance.

To achieve this, we carefully extracted and isolated layouts, templates, and other elements that remained consistent for both airlines. This process ensured that the core structure of the product could be easily maintained and reused while allowing for individualised skin or branding for each airline. By separating structure and skin, we achieved a flexible and scalable design system that could accommodate any number of airlines in the future without compromising on the product's integrity.

Car analogy: Extracting structure from the product

Redesigning Components

In the mobile web domain, we embarked on a comprehensive redesign effort, transforming both foundational styles and SASS components. Given the significant legacy code, we adopted a hybrid approach, combining principles of atomic design and component-based design.

This strategic approach allowed us to effectively streamline and improve our rebrand directives, resulting in a more cohesive and visually appealing user interface. By reimagining and updating the components, we ensured that the mobile web platform aligned seamlessly with the new brand guidelines and delivered an enhanced user experience.

Car analogy: Redesign car components

New model for Mobile Web

Following the redesign efforts, the newly rebranded components were integrated into the existing structure, forming a cohesive and unified model.

From a technical standpoint, we achieved this by building a source code for the mobile web that could exhibit polymorphic behaviour. This allowed the platform to dynamically adapt its appearance based on different branding requirements or airline-specific styles, ensuring a seamless and flexible user experience for each unique sub-product.

This approach also helped to maintain a consistent and standardised underlying structure. It not only simplified maintenance but also ensured a more efficient and scalable product that could evolve with the changing needs of the airlines and the industry.

Multiple products using structure of one product and multiple brands

Enabling iOS and Android UI team

Following multiple rounds of refinements and incorporating valuable feedback, we shared the refined directives with the iOS and Android teams.

Collaborating closely with both teams, I played a key role in enabling them to implement the product redesign using the set of directives provided. Throughout the process, we placed significant emphasis on maintaining consistency across all platforms, ensuring that our guidelines were followed uniformly.

This collaborative effort not only facilitated a smooth and efficient redesign process for both iOS and Android platforms but also ensured a seamless and unified user experience across all devices. By working closely with the teams and fostering consistent design implementation, we successfully achieved our goal of creating a consistent and cohesive user interface across all platforms.

Partnership with the QA

I partnered with the QA team to develop the 'Nakal' Ruby gem, enabling automated visual regression testing. This streamlined our visual QA process during development, ensuring quicker identification and resolution of discrepancies. The collaboration improved efficiency and visual consistency in the final product.
